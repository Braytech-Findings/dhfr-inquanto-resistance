# What the results mean — plain language

## The question

Does changing DHFR from WT to L28R affect TMP and 4-DTMP in the same way?

## The current answer

The compact classical pilot says **probably not in this simplified model**. Its
difference-in-differences is about `-0.055 Hartree`. In everyday words, changing
the protein changed the two ligand calculations by different amounts.

That is a clue, not proof. These small computer models do not include everything
that happens in a cell or body. Their interaction-energy values are not binding
free energies, resistance measurements, or treatment predictions.

## What the quantum data add

The WT_TMP quantum workflow was checked very carefully. All 1,818 measured
quantities matched the exact calculation, and repeated local measurements moved
toward the exact answer as the shot count grew.

The quantum data do **not** yet compare all four systems. The remote molecular
pilot timed out and returned no results. Therefore, the quantum portion cannot
yet answer the full mutation-and-ligand question.

## Why this is still important

The project now has a tested measurement method, honest uncertainty, real
prepared structures, and a clear list of missing work. That makes the next
experiment easier to plan and harder to misinterpret.
