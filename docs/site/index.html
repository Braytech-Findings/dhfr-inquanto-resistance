COMPUTER MODEL STUDY

Can one protein change affect two related molecules differently?

We built four simplified DHFR–ligand computer models, checked one quantum model deeply, and kept unfinished results clearly labeled.

The short answer

The classical pilot says “possibly.” In the simplified model, the difference-in-differences is about −0.055 Hartree. That means the L28R change affects the two ligands differently in this model.

The quantum study cannot answer the full four-system question yet. Only WT_TMP has a complete validated quantum estimator. This is not proof of drug resistance, binding, treatment value, or quantum advantage.
