# Scientific background

DHFR (dihydrofolate reductase) is an enzyme: a protein that helps cells carry out chemistry needed for growth. Trimethoprim (TMP) is a DHFR-targeting antibacterial drug. Mutations can alter a protein’s shape or electrostatic environment and can contribute to altered drug response. That possibility is a motivation for molecular and experimental research; this repository does not establish a clinical mechanism.

Computational chemistry uses mathematical models to estimate molecular properties. Quantum chemistry is relevant because electrons obey quantum mechanics. Quantum computing is a possible way to represent selected electronic models, but it does not automatically make every calculation faster or more accurate. Quantinuum InQuanto is specialized software used here to formulate quantum-chemistry workflows and circuits.

This project connects structural inputs, selected molecular models, classical electronic-structure methods, a small active-space Hamiltonian, and quantum-circuit measurements. It is an exploratory reproducibility project, not medical advice or a replacement for laboratory experiments.
